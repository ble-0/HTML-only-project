Franz Kafka

A prolific writer whose work remains unmatched


Kafka was a German-lamguage novelist and writer from Prague, widely deemed as a major figure of the 20th-century literature. Few of his works were published during is lifetime however attracted scant public attention as compared to the ones published after his death that made him rise to fame. A brief recap of his life is as follows:

  • 1883- Born into a middle class German-speaking Czech jewish family near the Old Town Square.
  • 1889- Attended elementary school and left during 1893.
  • 1901- Admitted to University whee he studied chemistry for two weeks and transitioned into law to get more time for German studies and art history classes.a year into the course he met Max Brod who became a close friend for life.
  • 1907- Got employed at the Assicurazioni Generali,an insurance company, where he worked for a year before he changed his workplace to the Worker's Accident INsurance Institute for the Kingdom of Bohemia.
  • 1908- Kafka's earliesst works were published which appeared in the first issue of the journal Hperion under the title Betrachtung, which translated to Contemplatiom.
  • 1912- Began wiritng his first novel The Man Who Disappeared which remained unfinished. It was however published by Brod after kafka's death and renamed to Amerika.
  • 1913- Kafka was able to publish his first book Contemplation with the help of his friend Brod.
  • 1924- Died with the cause being starvation which was brought about by his worsened laryngeal tuberculosis.
  • 1925- First instance of Kafka being translated to English when William A. Drake published "A Report for an Academy" in the New York Herald Tribune
  • 1999- A major component of the Franz Kafka Museum in Prague was first showcased in Barcelona.
  • 2001- the Franz Kafka Prize was established which is an annual literary award of the Franz Kafka Society and the city of Prague.
